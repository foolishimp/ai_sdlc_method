## Summary
The right home for this clarification is a child amendment to ADR-S-031, not a new standalone ADR in a different series. The issue is primarily saga supervision semantics: how F_H can cede bounded operational authority, where speculative branches are legal, what counts as the current accountability anchor, and when recovery is re-anchoring versus compensation.

This should become `ADR-S-031.1`, with a small follow-up cross-reference into ADR-S-015 because `rollback` is currently acceptable for local transaction language but too loose for saga-level accountability and external commit boundaries.

## Placement Recommendation

Create:

`ADR-S-031.1-delegated-authority-accountability-boundaries-and-speculative-branching.md`

Why it belongs under ADR-S-031:

- ADR-S-031 already owns supervisor, observer, relay, saga, and compensation semantics.
- The new clarification is about how authority is delegated within that supervisory saga.
- The key concepts are not primarily graph topology, dispatch compilation, or vector subtype schema.
- The central distinction is supervisory: "who may proceed autonomously, up to which boundary, and what kind of recovery remains possible after that boundary?"

Why it should not primarily live elsewhere:

- Not ADR-S-029: this is not mainly about the compiled dispatch intermediate.
- Not ADR-S-030.1: immutable lineage matters here, but the new issue is not vector typing; it is authority and recovery semantics over branches.
- Not ADR-S-015 as the owning ADR: transaction commit semantics are relevant, but the new rule is larger than one edge-local unit of work.

## Normative Scope It Should Own

The amendment should define these terms explicitly:

- `delegated authority`: bounded authority ceded by F_H to allow autonomous progression inside a mandate
- `accountability boundary`: the last ratified state from which the system may safely re-anchor
- `irreversibility boundary`: a boundary after which the effects are not cleanly reversible by local replay alone
- `speculative branch`: a predictive branch explored under delegated authority before an irreversibility boundary
- `abandoned branch`: a retained but non-authoritative branch that is no longer the active path
- `compensation`: forward recovery after an external or otherwise irreversible commitment

## Decision Shape

The ADR should say, normatively:

1. F_H may delegate bounded operational authority without ceding accountability.
2. Autonomous relays may continue within that delegated envelope until they hit a declared boundary condition.
3. Before an irreversibility boundary, speculative branching is allowed.
4. Unselected or failed speculative branches are abandoned operationally but retained historically.
5. Recovery before irreversible commitment is re-anchoring to the last accountable state, not history rewrite.
6. Recovery after irreversible commitment is compensation, never rollback.
7. Actions that cross a non-compensable or policy-sensitive external boundary require either live F_H approval or standing delegated policy authority.

## Why This Matters

This resolves a language gap that currently causes three different ideas to blur together:

- local transaction failure
- branch abandonment
- real-world rollback

Those are not the same operation.

Genesis is event-sourced and lineage-preserving. That means:

- speculative work can be cheap to abandon operationally
- historical work is not deleted
- the active head can move back to a prior accountability anchor
- once an external commit happens, the recovery model changes from re-anchoring to compensation

This is the clean way to describe predictive branching without importing fake rollback semantics from mutable workflow systems.

## Interaction With Existing ADRs

ADR-S-031 should own the supervisory rule:

- who delegates
- who remains accountable
- where autonomy stops
- what recovery mode applies

ADR-S-015 should then be tightened by cross-reference:

- keep `FAIL` / `ABORT` for edge-local non-commit
- avoid letting "rollback" imply deletion or global history reversion
- note that saga-level recovery after commitment is compensation, not rollback

ADR-S-030.1 remains consistent:

- abandoned branches remain in immutable lineage
- the system changes which branch is authoritative, not whether the branch existed

## Recommended Action

1. Write `ADR-S-031.1` as the owning clarification.
2. Define the six terms above explicitly and normatively.
3. Replace vague `rollback` language at saga scope with `re-anchor` versus `compensate`.
4. Add one short cross-reference note in ADR-S-015 after `ADR-S-031.1` is accepted.
5. Use one concrete example in the ADR: speculative branching before payment dispatch, then compensation after payment dispatch.
